# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-04-15

## Theme
Today’s strongest theme was **world models made more usable by explicit verification or hierarchy**. The best papers were not bigger simulators, but papers that changed how a planner checks, decomposes, or anchors its predictions.

## Short overview
This was a **good but narrow day**. I found **three papers worth saving**, with **two clear direct hits** and **one adjacent but useful 3D generation paper**. **World Action Verifier** is the strongest paper overall because it introduces a genuinely useful asymmetry: verifying world-model predictions by splitting them into plausibility and action reachability, then using extra video data and a sparse inverse model to check each piece. **Hierarchical Planning with Latent World Models** is the most directly useful for planning because it adds temporal hierarchy at inference time, over pretrained latent world models, without needing skill learning or a task-specific hierarchical policy. **3D-Fixer** is less central to world modeling, but it is a solid structured 3D generation paper because it replaces pose-search-heavy assembly with in-place completion anchored by fragmented geometry.

I also checked **CausalVAE as a Plug-in for World Models**. It is interesting, especially for intervention-aware counterfactual retrieval, but on current evidence it looks more like a promising structural add-on than a stronger end-to-end systems paper than the two world-model picks above, so I did not promote it into today’s saved set.

## Ranked papers

1. **World Action Verifier: Self-Improving World Models via Forward-Inverse Asymmetry**  
   - Verdict: **Must read**  
   - Role: **Directly relevant**  
   - Why it matters: It turns world-model self-improvement into a verification problem with a concrete mechanism, instead of vague uncertainty scoring or more brute-force data collection.

2. **Hierarchical Planning with Latent World Models**  
   - Verdict: **Highly relevant**  
   - Role: **Directly relevant**  
   - Why it matters: It inserts temporal abstraction directly into latent-space MPC and shows a real payoff on non-greedy long-horizon control, without requiring learned skills or hierarchical policies.

3. **3D-Fixer: Coarse-to-Fine In-place Completion for 3D Scenes from a Single Image**  
   - Verdict: **Useful**  
   - Role: **Adjacent but useful inspiration**  
   - Why it matters: It uses partial observed geometry as a spatial anchor and completes assets in place, which is a cleaner structural interface than generate-then-align pipelines.

## Most relevant to current work
**World Action Verifier** is the most relevant paper today. The key reusable idea is that world-model reliability can be improved by verifying easier subproblems, plausible future state and action-consistent reachability, instead of treating uncertainty estimation as one opaque scalar problem.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Mechanism impact:** WAV is a strong citation for the claim that world-model robustness can come from structured self-verification, not only larger backbones or more exploration heuristics.
- **Planning impact:** HWM is a useful reference if you need to argue that long-horizon planning can be improved by temporal abstraction at inference time, even with frozen pretrained world models.
- **3D generation impact:** 3D-Fixer is a decent citation against alignment-heavy scene assembly pipelines and for using partial geometry as a hard spatial anchor.
- **Novelty threat:** Low to moderate. WAV and HWM overlap with broad world-model planning themes already in the repo, but both add distinct mechanisms rather than repackaging the same idea.
- **Baseline impact:** WAV is the strongest candidate for related work or future baseline thinking around active data collection and verification. HWM is a plausible planning baseline or citation for hierarchical latent MPC. 3D-Fixer is more citation material than a direct baseline.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The best work today did not try to solve everything end to end. WAV decomposes prediction quality into two easier checks and uses that structure for self-improvement. HWM decomposes planning across timescales in a shared latent space so the planner can reason long horizon without optimizing one huge primitive-action sequence. 3D-Fixer decomposes scene generation into geometry-anchored instance completion rather than fragile retrieve-and-align loops. Overall, this was a day for papers that make intermediate structure carry operational weight.
