# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-04-17

## Theme
Today’s strongest theme was **planning that gets easier by changing the interface**, not by making the base model bigger. The best papers either move planning into a better latent/action abstraction or change how world-model quality is measured.

## Short overview
This was a **selective but worthwhile day**. I found **three papers worth saving**. **World-Value-Action Model** is the most directly relevant new paper because it explicitly couples a world model, a value model, and latent-space action inference for long-horizon VLA behavior. **Compositional Planning with Jumpy World Models** is the cleanest conceptual paper of the set: it treats temporally extended policy composition as the planning object and builds multi-timescale predictive models around that choice. **Behavior Consistency in Text-Based World Models** is less directly aligned with robotics or 3D generation, but it is methodologically useful because it argues that next-state accuracy is the wrong target when the real goal is preserving downstream agent behavior.

I also checked **HY-World 2.0**, **GaussianGPT**, and **Goal2Skill**. HY-World 2.0 looks like a large integrated system push rather than a tight new mechanism. GaussianGPT is interesting as an explicit autoregressive 3D scene representation, but from current access it looks more like a promising generative formulation than a stronger methodological paper than the three saved here. Goal2Skill looks directionally useful, but too much of the gain currently seems to come from a fairly standard planner-executor split plus recovery loop, so I did not promote it today.

## Most relevant to current work
**World-Value-Action Model** is the most relevant paper today. The key reusable idea is not just “world model + value model,” but that latent inference can concentrate search onto dynamically feasible, high-value futures when direct action-space search becomes combinatorially brittle.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Planning framing:** WAV is a useful citation if you want to argue that VLA systems need an internal planning interface instead of direct one-shot action emission.
- **Temporal abstraction framing:** Jumpy World Models is a strong citation for treating policy composition as the primitive and learning predictive models over temporally extended actions.
- **Evaluation framing:** Behavior Consistency is a good reference against over-trusting exact-match next-state metrics for downstream planning or offline model evaluation.
- **Novelty threat:** Low. These papers overlap with themes already in the repo, but each pushes a distinct interface: latent feasibility inference, multi-timescale policy occupancy modeling, and behavior-aligned world-model training.
- **Baseline impact:** WAV and Jumpy World Models are the stronger related-work items. Behavior Consistency is more of a methodological lens than a direct baseline.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The interesting papers today all reject a naive end-to-end interface. WAV says direct action prediction is the wrong search space for long-horizon VLA control. Jumpy World Models says primitive actions are the wrong temporal unit when planning should compose reusable policies. Behavior Consistency says exact state prediction is the wrong training target if the goal is to preserve downstream decisions. Different domains, same lesson: better internal interfaces can matter more than more capacity.
