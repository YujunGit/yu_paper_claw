# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-04-26

## Theme
Today’s worthwhile thread was **putting stronger structure directly into generative or temporal modeling**, instead of asking a generic model to recover it implicitly. The best candidate repurposes native 3D generative priors for layout reasoning. The runner-up extends a strong 3D generator into 4D with a sparse temporal mechanism that is simple enough to matter.

## Short overview
This was a **selective, slightly thin day**. I found **two papers worth keeping** and one adjacent paper worth remembering but not promoting heavily.

The strongest direct pick is **Repurposing 3D Generative Model for Autoregressive Layout Generation**. It is interesting because it treats layout as a native 3D generative process with explicit physical and geometric constraints, rather than as text generation plus cleanup.

The second pick is **Sculpt4D: Generating 4D Shapes via Sparse-Attention Diffusion Transformers**. It is less conceptually deep, but the mechanism is concrete and potentially reusable: keep a first-frame identity anchor, add time-decaying sparse attention, and make 4D adaptation computationally tolerable.

I also looked at recent papers on agentic 3D scene synthesis, unified 3D generation-understanding, world-model acceleration, and neuro-symbolic action modeling. Most were either too packaging-heavy, too benchmark/system-oriented, or not strong enough on mechanism to justify today’s shortlist.

## Most relevant to current work
**Repurposing 3D Generative Model for Autoregressive Layout Generation** is the best fit. The important idea is to move layout reasoning into the model’s native geometric space and let autoregression provide controllability, editing, and completion almost for free.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **3D generation framing:** LaviGen is a useful citation against purely text-native layout pipelines that do not actually model 3D physical relations.
- **4D generation framing:** Sculpt4D is useful if you need a concrete example of adapting a strong pretrained 3D model to 4D with a targeted temporal mechanism instead of rebuilding from scratch.
- **Novelty threat:** Low. These papers overlap with controllable generation and structural priors, but neither appears to invalidate a broader agenda around compositional or structured world/generative models.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The best paper today, LaviGen, is strong because it changes the representational interface: layout generation is performed in native 3D space with autoregressive scene updates, rather than as symbolic text that later gets repaired. Sculpt4D is more incremental, but still useful because its temporal mechanism is crisp and transferable: anchor identity to the first frame, sparsify time interactions by distance, and inherit spatial competence from a pretrained 3D generator rather than relearning everything from limited 4D data.
