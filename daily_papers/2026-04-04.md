# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-04-04

## Theme
Today’s worthwhile thread was **explicit control interfaces inside learned systems**: one paper makes multi-entity control in world models more concrete, and one paper makes visual features steerable without giving up their visual character.

## Short overview
This was a **thin but not empty** day. I found **one strong direct fit** and **one adjacent paper worth keeping**. The strongest paper is **ActionParty**, because it addresses a real structural failure in interactive world models—binding the right action to the right subject—using persistent per-subject latent state rather than prompt-level wishful thinking. The adjacent but useful paper is **Steerable Visual Representations**, which is not a world-model paper, but it offers a clean recipe for promptable visual features via early language-to-vision fusion.

I did **not** add **Generative World Renderer** today. It looks potentially useful as dataset/infrastructure for controllable inverse/forward rendering, but on first pass it is more of a strong resource paper than a must-read methodological contribution for your current interests.

## Most relevant to current work
**ActionParty** is the clear pick. The important part is not “multi-player video games”; it is the architectural choice to give each controllable entity a persistent latent state and make binding an explicit design problem.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Framing impact:** Good support for the claim that explicit intermediate state matters when several entities must remain separately controllable over time.
- **Related-work impact:** ActionParty is worth citing if you need examples of world models where modular latent state is introduced to fix a specific control failure. SteerViT is useful for representation-control arguments, not for direct world-model baselines.
- **Novelty threat:** Low direct collision. ActionParty overlaps at the design-principle level, not the exact problem level.
- **Baseline impact:** Limited immediate baseline pressure unless you are building multi-entity interactive video generation. More useful as a mechanism and framing reference.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The best paper today earned its structure claim. ActionParty does not merely say “multi-agent” and hope the generator figures it out; it creates persistent subject-specific state and uses explicit masking plus spatial grounding to keep actions attached to the right entities. SteerViT works at a different layer of the stack, but the shared lesson is similar: if you want controllability, put the control interface inside the representation, not only in the prompt.
