# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-03-30

## Theme
What makes a latent state actually useful: straighten it for planning, discretize it for symbolic structure, or constrain only the missing geometry.

## Short overview
Today’s strongest paper is **Temporal Straightening for Latent Planning**. It makes a narrow but real representation claim: planning gets easier when latent trajectories are locally straight enough that Euclidean distance better matches reachable-path distance. That is a cleaner mechanism than many recent “better world-model representation” papers. **Discrete World Models via Regularization** is smaller-scale but conceptually sharp: it learns Boolean latent states without leaning on reconstruction, instead pushing for entropy, independence, and sparse local action effects. **RelaxFlow** is the best 3D-generation paper in the batch, though more as a controllable-geometry interface idea than as a broad conceptual leap; its useful contribution is separating rigid observation preservation from softer text-driven completion of occluded structure.

## Most relevant to current work
**Temporal Straightening for Latent Planning** is the best direct hit. The paper is not broad, but it is honest about what it is fixing: latent geometry for planning, not “world understanding” in general.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Framing impact:** Useful support for the idea that structure in representations should improve optimization geometry or transition semantics, not just produce nicer features.
- **Related-work impact:** Temporal Straightening is relevant for planning-oriented latent learning; DWMR is relevant if you need explicit/discrete latent-state references; RelaxFlow is more useful for controllable 3D completion framing than for world-model novelty.
- **Novelty threat:** Low. None of these looks like a direct collision with broader structured world-model or neurosymbolic generation claims.
- **Baseline impact:** Moderate for latent planning papers. Temporal Straightening is worth remembering as a representation-level comparison; DWMR is worth citing when discrete state abstractions come up.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The better papers today all make a specific claim about what the intermediate state should do. Temporal Straightening says the latent should make planning geometry easier to optimize. DWMR says the state should expose discrete factors and sparse local transitions rather than inherit decoder-friendly entanglement. RelaxFlow says controllable generation under occlusion needs asymmetric control: preserve what is observed rigidly, but relax the semantic constraint on unseen structure. The shared lesson is good: latent structure earns attention when it changes the feasible inference problem, not when it merely sounds interpretable.
