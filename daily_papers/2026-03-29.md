# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-03-29

## Theme
Structure only matters if it constrains the rollout interface: sparse kinematic milestones, collision-aware 3D composition, and explicit state supervision.

## Short overview
Today’s strongest paper is **Beyond Dense Futures: World Models as Structured Planners for Robotic Manipulation**. It makes a concrete representational choice: instead of dense video rollout or vague latent planning, it predicts sparse, kinematically meaningful structured frames and then maps them to actions. That is a real mechanism, not branding. **Interact3D** is the best 3D-generation paper in the batch because it turns compositional generation into a registration-and-optimization problem with explicit collision penalties, instead of just hoping a generative prior preserves object-object relations. **WildWorld** is less about method novelty than infrastructure, but it is one of the cleaner recent dataset papers for state-aware interactive world modeling because it exposes explicit state, not just pixels plus keyboard inputs. **Know3D** is more speculative: interesting as a controllability pattern for unseen geometry, but still closer to knowledge-guided hallucination repair than to a deep structural advance.

## Ranked papers

1. **Beyond Dense Futures: World Models as Structured Planners for Robotic Manipulation**  
   - Verdict: **Must read**  
   - Role: **Directly relevant**  
   - Why it matters: It replaces dense future prediction with sparse, physically grounded milestones that explicitly bridge world-model foresight and low-level robot control.

2. **Interact3D: Compositional 3D Generation of Interactive Objects**  
   - Verdict: **Highly relevant**  
   - Role: **Directly relevant / methodological inspiration**  
   - Why it matters: It treats compositional 3D generation as structured geometric alignment plus SDF-based collision-aware optimization, which is much more convincing than generic “interactive 3D” phrasing.

3. **WildWorld: A Large-Scale Dataset for Dynamic World Modeling with Actions and Explicit State toward Generative ARPG**  
   - Verdict: **Useful**  
   - Role: **Citation / benchmark infrastructure**  
   - Why it matters: It is a good reference if you need to argue that world-model evaluation should include explicit state alignment rather than only pixel realism or prompt following.

4. **Know3D: Prompting 3D Generation with Knowledge from Vision-Language Models**  
   - Verdict: **Useful**  
   - Role: **Adjacent inspiration**  
   - Why it matters: It offers an interesting bridge from VLM semantics to controllable unseen-view geometry, though the mechanism still feels more like guided prior transfer than a cleanly grounded 3D structure model.

## Most relevant to current work
**Beyond Dense Futures / StructVLA** is the best direct hit. It makes a sharper claim than many recent robot world-model papers: the predictive interface should expose sparse physically meaningful waypoints instead of dense visual futures or opaque latent rollouts.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Framing impact:** Strong support for the claim that structure earns its keep when it appears at the planning interface or transition substrate, not when it is only added as an auxiliary caption or downstream reranker.
- **Related-work impact:** StructVLA is useful for structured planning in embodied control; Interact3D is useful for compositional 3D generation with explicit geometric constraints; WildWorld is useful when arguing for state-aware world-model data and evaluation.
- **Novelty threat:** Low to moderate. StructVLA is closest in spirit if your work claims structured world-model planning; the others feel more complementary than threatening.
- **Baseline impact:** Moderate. StructVLA looks worth keeping as a concrete baseline or comparison point for sparse-foresight planning. WildWorld/WildBench may also matter if you need richer state-aware evaluation references.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The common thread today is that the better papers stop pretending dense realism is enough. StructVLA compresses foresight into sparse kinematic milestones that are closer to how control should actually be guided. Interact3D constrains composition with registration and explicit collision penalties instead of hoping a giant generator preserves object relations. WildWorld contributes explicit state and alignment metrics so “interactive world modeling” is not judged only by pretty videos. Know3D is weaker but still useful as evidence that controllable generation of unseen structure may benefit from importing richer semantic priors through a better intermediate interface.
