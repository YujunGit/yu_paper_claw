# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-04-01

## Theme
Autoregression is becoming interesting again in 3D only when the representation is explicit enough to make stepwise construction actually meaningful.

## Short overview
Today was a selective day. I only found **two** recent papers worth writing up. The stronger one for your interests is **GaussianGPT**, not because autoregression is automatically better than diffusion, but because it pairs autoregression with an explicit tokenized Gaussian scene representation that makes completion, outpainting, and incremental scene growth more natural. **AssetFormer** is narrower and more application-bound, but still useful as a clean example of real compositionality: assets are generated as ordered sequences of reusable modules with constrained attributes, not just as monolithic geometry with “modular” branding.

I did **not** find a third recent paper strong enough to justify adding. There were adjacent candidates, but most either looked too benchmark-driven, too interface-thin, or too hype-heavy relative to the actual mechanism.

## Most relevant to current work
**GaussianGPT** is the best fit today. The main reason is not the autoregressive label itself, but the explicit interface between scene structure and generation. If you care about controllable, decomposable, or planner-friendly 3D state, this is more interesting than another end-to-end diffusion paper.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Framing impact:** Useful support for the claim that 3D generation gets more interesting when intermediate state is explicit and editable rather than only implicit and denoised.
- **Related-work impact:** GaussianGPT is relevant for structured 3D scene generation; AssetFormer is useful citation material when arguing for true compositional generation via reusable parts and constrained decoding.
- **Novelty threat:** Low to moderate. These papers overlap with your interests in structured generation and modularity, but neither looks like a direct novelty collision.
- **Baseline impact:** Moderate. GaussianGPT is worth remembering if you need an autoregressive 3D baseline or a representation argument; AssetFormer is more of a mechanism citation than a must-run baseline.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The interesting thread today is not “autoregression is back.” It is that autoregression only becomes compelling when the representation gives it something meaningful to predict. GaussianGPT works because it tokenizes explicit Gaussian scene state and separates geometry from appearance, making stepwise scene growth plausible. AssetFormer works, within a narrower domain, because it models assets as sequences of reusable modules with constrained attributes and valid decoding. The transferable lesson is that controllability comes less from the sampler family and more from whether the intermediate state is explicit, editable, and aligned with downstream use.
