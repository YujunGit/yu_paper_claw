# Daily Paper Digest — 2026-04-03

## Theme
The strongest recent papers were not generic scale-ups, but papers that make a sharper claim about **where structure should live**: in the world representation, in the inference process, or in the architecture itself.

## Short overview
Today was selective. I found **three** recent papers worth adding, and I rejected several adjacent candidates that felt too benchmark-forward or too scale-driven relative to their actual mechanism. The best fit for your interests is **WorldFlow3D**, because it treats controllable 3D world generation as a problem of transporting between explicit 3D distributions rather than just denoising latent noise. The most conceptually important paper is **Functorial Neural Architectures from Higher Inductive Types**: it is much more theoretical, but it makes a rare attempt to explain compositional generalization failure as an architectural mismatch rather than just a data problem. **Thinking Deeper, Not Longer** sits between them: less directly tied to generation, but methodologically useful if you care about inference-time depth, reusable reasoning cores, and mechanism over packaging.

I did **not** add ABot-PhysWorld today. It is topical and probably useful as a citation if needed, but on first pass it reads more like a very large embodied video model plus post-training and a new benchmark than a clean conceptual advance.

## Ranked papers

1. **WorldFlow3D: Flowing Through 3D Distributions for Unbounded World Generation**  
   - Verdict: **Highly relevant**  
   - Role: **Directly relevant**  
   - Why it matters: It argues that explicit 3D distribution transport can be a better scaffold for controllable unbounded world generation than standard conditional denoising pipelines.

2. **Functorial Neural Architectures from Higher Inductive Types**  
   - Verdict: **Useful**  
   - Role: **Adjacent but useful inspiration**  
   - Why it matters: It makes a strong structural claim that compositional generalization is an architectural property and tries to enforce it by construction, not just by training tricks.

3. **Thinking Deeper, Not Longer: Depth-Recurrent Transformers for Compositional Generalization**  
   - Verdict: **Useful**  
   - Role: **Conceptual inspiration**  
   - Why it matters: It gives a concrete recipe for separating parameter count from inference-time reasoning depth, with a clearer mechanism than most generic “test-time thinking” papers.

## Most relevant to current work
**WorldFlow3D** is the best fit today. The key point is not just that it is another 3D generator, but that it puts structure and controllability at the level of the world representation and transport path. That is much closer to your interests than another polished black-box generator.

## Novelty / framing / baseline impact
- **Framing impact:** Strong support for the claim that structure should be explicit and operational, not only implied by prompts or latent denoising.
- **Related-work impact:** WorldFlow3D is relevant for controllable 3D world generation; Functorial Neural Architectures is useful if you need a more principled argument about why compositionality may require architectural constraints; Depth-Recurrent Transformers is useful for reasoning-core discussion.
- **Novelty threat:** Low direct collision. These papers overlap more at the mechanism/design-principle level than at the exact problem statement level.
- **Baseline impact:** Moderate. WorldFlow3D is the only one here that looks like a plausible direct baseline/citation anchor for 3D generation. The other two matter more for conceptual framing than for immediate baseline replication.

## One-paragraph takeaway
The common thread today is that good papers are making a commitment about structure instead of merely naming it. WorldFlow3D places structure in an explicit 3D transport process with controllable layout and attribute conditions. Depth-Recurrent Transformers place it in the inference computation itself by separating reasoning depth from parameter count. Functorial Neural Architectures go further and claim compositionality must be baked into architecture-level algebraic structure to hold reliably at all. Different levels, same lesson: controllability and generalization usually need an explicit interface, not just a larger model.
